The 50/30/20 Budget Rule - Complete Guide

The simplest budgeting method that actually works

What is the 50/30/20 Rule?

The 50/30/20 rule is a simple budgeting framework that divides your after-tax income into three categories:

50%

Needs

30%

Wants

20%

Savings & Debt

Category Breakdown:

üè† 50% - Needs (Essential Expenses)

  • Rent or mortgage payments
  • Groceries and essential food
  • Utilities (electricity, water, gas)
  • Health insurance and medical care
  • Minimum debt payments
  • Basic transportation

üéØ 30% - Wants (Lifestyle Choices)

  • Dining out and entertainment
  • Streaming services and subscriptions
  • Travel and vacations
  • Hobbies and personal care
  • Brand-name clothing
  • Any non-essential purchases

üí∞ 20% - Savings & Debt

  • Emergency fund contributions
  • Retirement accounts (401k, IRA)
  • Extra debt payments above minimum
  • Investments and savings goals
  • Education funds

Example: $4,000 Monthly Income

Category Percentage Amount Examples
Needs 50% $2,000 Rent, groceries, utilities
Wants 30% $1,200 Dining out, entertainment, hobbies
Savings & Debt 20% $800 Emergency fund, retirement, extra payments

Getting Started Today:

  1. Calculate your monthly after-tax income
  2. Track your current spending for 30 days
  3. Categorize expenses into needs, wants, and savings
  4. Adjust spending to fit the 50/30/20 framework
  5. Automate your savings and bill payments
